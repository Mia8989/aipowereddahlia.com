5 Tools to Track If AI Search Engines Are Citing Your Business

You published structured content. You added schema markup. You built FAQ sections, cited authoritative sources, and followed every GEO best practice in the playbook. But here is the question nobody talks about: how do you actually know if it is working?

Traditional SEO tools were not built for this. Google Search Console tracks impressions and clicks from search results pages. Ahrefs and SEMrush monitor keyword rankings and backlinks. None of them can tell you whether ChatGPT mentioned your brand in its last 10,000 responses, or whether Perplexity cited your blog post when a prospect asked about your exact service category.

40-60% of cited sources in AI-generated responses rotate every month (Superlines, Q1 2026)

That stat should concern you. AI citation is not a set-and-forget game. The sources these models reference shift constantly as new content emerges, training data refreshes, and retrieval algorithms evolve. If you are not actively monitoring your AI visibility, you are flying blind.

Here are the five tools we have evaluated for tracking GEO performance across AI search platforms, along with guidance on which one fits your situation.

1. Profound: Broadest Platform Coverage

Best For

Enterprises needing cross-platform AI visibility at scale

Tracks 10+ AI engines including ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, Copilot, DeepSeek, Grok, and Meta AI.

Profound stands out for sheer coverage. While most tools track three or four AI platforms, Profound monitors more than ten, giving you the most complete picture of where your brand appears (and where it does not) across the AI search ecosystem.

The platform uses real user-facing queries rather than simulated prompts, which means the data reflects actual AI behavior in production. You can track brand mentions, URL citations, competitor share of voice, and sentiment across all monitored platforms from a single dashboard.

For organizations that serve multiple markets or compete across several verticals, this breadth matters. An AI engine that ignores you on one platform might cite you consistently on another, and without cross-platform tracking, you would never know the difference.

2. SE Ranking: GEO Meets Traditional SEO

Best For

Teams that want GEO data integrated with their existing SEO workflow

Connects AI search exposure to keyword rankings, traffic trends, backlink authority, and competitor movement in one platform.

SE Ranking's approach is practical: rather than treating GEO as a separate discipline, it layers AI citation tracking directly into the SEO metrics you already monitor. You can see how your keyword clusters perform in traditional search alongside their AI search visibility, which makes it easier to spot patterns and prioritize content updates.

25% predicted drop in traditional search volume as users shift to AI answer engines (WSI World, 2026)

This integration matters because traditional SEO and GEO are not independent. Pages that rank well in Google often get cited more frequently by AI search engines, since many retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) systems use search rankings as a signal. SE Ranking lets you see both sides of that relationship without switching between tools.

3. Otterly AI: Most Accessible Entry Point

Best For

Small teams and startups getting started with GEO monitoring

Simple mention tracking across ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, AI Mode, Gemini, and Copilot. Starts at $29 to $39 per month.

Not every business needs enterprise-grade AI monitoring. Otterly AI is the most practical starting point for teams that want visibility into AI citations without committing to a large platform investment. The setup is straightforward: enter your brand name, target keywords, and competitors, and Otterly begins tracking mentions across six major AI platforms.

The dashboards are intuitive, the learning curve is minimal, and the pricing makes it accessible for businesses with limited marketing budgets. If you are a solo operator or a team of two to five people, this is likely the right first step before scaling into a larger platform.

The tradeoff is depth. Otterly provides clean, directional data on whether AI platforms are mentioning you, but it does not offer the granular citation analysis or content optimization recommendations that enterprise tools include. For most small businesses, that tradeoff is worth the significantly lower price.

4. ZipTie: Simplified AI Success Scoring

Best For

Teams that want one metric instead of multiple dashboards

Proprietary AI Success Score synthesizes mention frequency, sentiment, and citation strength into a single trackable number.

ZipTie takes a different approach to GEO analytics. Instead of presenting raw mention counts and citation URLs, it calculates a proprietary AI Success Score that combines three signals: how often AI engines mention you, what they say about you (sentiment), and how strongly they cite your content (direct URL references versus general brand mentions).

This simplification is useful for reporting and executive communication. Rather than explaining the difference between a mention and a citation across seven platforms, you can track a single score over time and communicate progress in terms leadership can immediately understand.

ZipTie also provides competitive scoring, so you can benchmark your AI Success Score against direct competitors. If your score drops while a competitor's rises, that is an early warning signal that their content strategy is displacing yours in AI responses.

2-7 domains cited per AI-generated response on average, compared to Google's 10 blue links

5. Semrush AI Visibility: Enterprise Grade with a Free Tier

Best For

Large agencies and enterprises already invested in the Semrush ecosystem

First comprehensive AI search monitoring backed by a decade of search visibility infrastructure. Free AI Search Visibility Checker available.

Semrush entered the GEO tracking space with the advantage of existing infrastructure. Their AI Visibility toolkit integrates directly with your existing Semrush projects, which means you can see AI citation data alongside your organic rankings, content gaps, and backlink profiles without migrating platforms.

The free AI Search Visibility Checker is worth noting. It provides a basic snapshot of your domain's AI search presence without requiring a paid subscription. For teams evaluating whether GEO tracking is worth the investment, this free tool offers a low-risk way to see what the data looks like before committing.

For agencies managing multiple client accounts, the ability to scale AI visibility tracking across portfolios within a tool they already use is the primary selling point. No additional vendor relationships, no separate login, no data export gymnastics.

How to Choose the Right Tool

The right tool depends on three factors: your team size, your current tooling, and what you plan to do with the data.

  • Solo operators or small teams (under 5 people): Start with Otterly AI. The price is right, the setup is fast, and you will have actionable data within a day.
  • Teams already using Semrush: Add the AI Visibility toolkit. No new vendor, no new learning curve, and the free tier lets you test before buying.
  • Marketing teams that want unified SEO + GEO: SE Ranking is the strongest option for seeing how traditional and AI search performance interact.
  • Executives who need clean reporting: ZipTie's single-score approach makes progress easy to communicate without drowning leadership in platform-specific metrics.
  • Enterprises tracking across 10+ AI engines: Profound is the only platform with this breadth of coverage. If you need to know how your brand appears on DeepSeek, Grok, and Meta AI alongside the major platforms, this is the tool.

The 5 GEO Metrics That Actually Matter

Regardless of which tool you choose, focus on these five metrics:

  1. Citation frequency: How often do AI engines link directly to your content? This is the most concrete measure of GEO success.
  2. Mention rate: How often does your brand name appear in AI responses, even without a direct link? This indicates brand awareness within the AI knowledge graph.
  3. Share of voice: What percentage of relevant queries result in your brand being cited versus competitors? This is the competitive metric that matters most.
  4. Sentiment: When AI engines mention you, do they describe your brand positively, neutrally, or negatively? You cannot control AI output, but you can influence it through the content you publish.
  5. Source rotation: Are the same pages getting cited consistently, or does the AI keep pulling from different parts of your site? Consistent citation of key pages indicates strong topical authority.

Traditional SEO gives you rankings. GEO tracking gives you visibility into the conversations AI is having about your brand when you are not in the room.

What to Do After You Start Tracking

Data without action is just a dashboard. Once you have GEO tracking in place, use the insights to drive three types of decisions:

  • Content updates: If a page was cited last month but dropped off this month, refresh it. AI models favor recent, comprehensive content. A quarterly update cycle keeps your best pages in rotation.
  • Gap analysis: If competitors are getting cited for queries where you have equal or better content, investigate why. Common causes include weaker schema markup, fewer authoritative backlinks, or content that does not directly answer the query format AI models prefer.
  • New content planning: Track which queries generate AI responses that cite zero results from your domain. These are your highest-value content opportunities because no competitor in your space owns that AI real estate yet.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I know if AI search engines are citing my business?

Traditional SEO tools like Google Search Console and Ahrefs cannot track AI citations because AI search engines do not generate standard search impressions or clicks. You need specialized GEO tracking tools such as Profound, SE Ranking, Otterly AI, ZipTie, or Semrush AI Visibility that monitor AI-generated responses across platforms like ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, and Claude for mentions and citations of your brand and URLs.

What is the difference between an AI mention and an AI citation?

An AI mention occurs when an AI search engine references your brand name or content in its response without linking to your site. An AI citation is when the AI explicitly includes a URL pointing back to your website. Citations are the more valuable metric because they drive direct traffic and demonstrate that the AI considers your content authoritative enough to reference as a source.

How much do GEO tracking tools cost?

Pricing varies widely. Otterly AI starts at $29 to $39 per month, making it the most accessible entry point. Semrush offers a free AI Search Visibility Checker for basic checks. Enterprise platforms like Profound and SE Ranking typically start at several hundred dollars per month depending on the number of queries and competitors tracked. Most tools offer free trials or limited free tiers.

How often should I check my AI search visibility?

At minimum, review your AI citation metrics weekly. Research shows that 40 to 60% of cited sources in AI-generated responses rotate month over month, which means your visibility can shift quickly. Set up automated alerts for significant changes and run a full competitive analysis monthly.
